Rules

The project presented is for a 39-inch long sword. The long sword found here is constructed very similarly to the short sword found in the book Battle Gaming: The New American Sport. In this instruction set, you will learn the “box” method of constructing a punch protector/rip resist. The box construction system has been around since the mid 1990’s. It has proven to be very reliable at increasing the longevity of the tip of the sword.

Here is a quick summary of design compliance with the major battle gaming systems. The finished project should, if built correctly, be able to pass through weapons inspection.

Table 1: Rules Compliance of the Long Sword Project

Dagorhir Handheld Blue Green

Remember that even if the finished product conforms to the rules of any given battle gaming system, you will still need to seek approval of the weapon from an appointed referee. The referee will judge the weapon on not only its design, but also the quality of construction. The most difficult part to get right will be the thrusting tip. It must compress, without folding sideways. If the weapon is poorly crafted, this may become a problem. In this case, some game systems will allow you to use the weapon without thrusting. You will need to check with your local referee.
