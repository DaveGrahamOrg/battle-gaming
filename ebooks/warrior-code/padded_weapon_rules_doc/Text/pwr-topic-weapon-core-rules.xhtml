Weapon Core Rules

Semi-Ridged Cores – The core material for a weapon must be ridged enough that it will not bend more than 45 degrees when the lower end of the striking surface hits a surface at full force.

No Metal Cores – The use of metal cores is prohibited for cores in padded weapons. Small metallic weights are permitted in the hilt of a padded weapon.

Wood Cores – The use of bamboo and palm plant wood such as rattan is permitted for cores in padded weapons. Tree wood, both hardwood and softwood, is prohibited for the use of padded weapon cores but may be used as weight in the hilt of a padded weapon.
