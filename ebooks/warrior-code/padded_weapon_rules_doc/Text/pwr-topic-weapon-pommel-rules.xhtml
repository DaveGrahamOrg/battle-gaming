Weapon Pommel Rules

Minimum Pommel Width – The end off all weapon pommels must pass the Larger than an Eye Socket rule.

Minimum Pommel Base – All pommels must be mounted on a foam base that consists of a non-striking surface that circles the core.  The foam base must be no less than 1-inch in length.

Minimum Pommel Padding for Light and Normal-Mass Weapons – The minimum amount of closed cell foam required on the end of a pommel for a light or normal-mass weapon is 1 inch.

Minimum Pommel Padding for Heavy-Mass Weapons – The minimum amount of closed cell foam required on the end of a pommel for a heavy-mass weapon is 1½ inches.

Minimum Pommel Side Padding – The side of a weapon pommel must conform to the minimum non-striking surface padding for the weapon’s classification.

Pommel Punch Protector – The use of a punch protector in a pommel is encouraged but optional.

Pommel Rip Resist – The use of a rip resistant layer in a pommel is encouraged but optional.

Minimum Spiked Pommel Padding – The construction of a spiked pommel on a handheld weapon must conform to the standards of a sharp point striking surface for the weapon’s classification.

Minimum Strike Legal Surface for Spiked Pommels – Any non-twirling handheld weapon with a spiked pommel must have a minimum of 6-inches of Strike Legal surface adjacent to the end of the pommel.

Minimum Grip Padding for Spiked Pommel Weapons – Any weapon with a spiked pommel must have a minimum of ⅜ inch of closed cell foam covering the grip of the weapon.
