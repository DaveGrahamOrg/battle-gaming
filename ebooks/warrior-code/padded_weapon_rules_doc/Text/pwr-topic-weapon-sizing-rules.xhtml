Weapon Sizing Rules

Larger Than An Eye Socket – A light-mass weapon is considered safe against an eye socket when it passes the Two-Inch Hole Rule on all parts.  A normal-mass handheld weapon is considered safe against an eye socket when it passes the Two-Inch Hole Rule on all parts.  A heavy-mass handheld weapon is considered safe against an eye socket when it passes the Two and One-Half Inch Hole Rule.  A missile weapon is considered safe against an eye socket whet it passes the Two and One-Half Inch Hole Rule.

Two-Inch Hole Rule – An item is considered safe against an eye socket when it will not pass easily through a 2-inch diameter hole and will not protrude more than ½ inch through a 2-inch diameter hole.

Two and One-Half Inch Hole Rule – An item is considered safe against the eye socket when it will not pass easily through a 2½-inch diameter hole and will not protrude more than ½ inch through a 2½-inch diameter hole.

Two and a Half Inch Curvature Rule – In unhelmeted combat, the smallest weapon permitted on the battlefield is a 2½-inch diameter sphere.  The rounded end of any weapon may not be tighter than a 2½-inch diameter curve.
