Weapon Blade or Bludgeon Rules

Minimum Padding Requirement for a Light-Mass Weapon Striking Surface – All striking surfaces on a light-mass weapon must be padded to a minimum of 1 inch of closed cell foam.

Minimum Padding Requirement for a Normal-Mass Weapon Striking Surface – All striking surfaces on a normal-mass weapon must be padded to a minimum of 1 inch of closed cell foam.

Minimum Padding Requirement for a Heavy-Mass Weapon Striking Surface – All striking surfaces on a heavy-mass weapon must be padded to a minimum of 1½ inch of closed cell foam.

Minimum Padding Requirement for a Light-Mass Weapon Non-striking Surface – All non-striking surfaces on a light-mass weapon must be padded to a minimum of ⅜ inch of closed cell foam.

Minimum Padding Requirement for a Normal-Mass Weapon Non-striking Surface – All non-striking surfaces on a normal-mass weapon must be padded to a minimum of ½ inch of closed cell foam.

Minimum Padding Requirement for a Heavy-Mass Weapon Non-striking Surface – All non-striking surfaces on a heavy-mass weapon must be padded to a minimum of ¾ inch of closed cell foam.

Minimum Striking Surface Thickness – The striking surface of all padded weapon blades and bludgeons must be at least 1¼ inches in thickness.

Minimum Striking Surface Width for Light and Normal Mass Weapons – The minimum width of all light and normal mass blades and bludgeons shall be not less than 2½ inches.  The blade or bludgeon must meet the minimum padding requirements for all striking surface.  The end of the blade or bludgeon must conform to the Larger than an Eye Socket rule.

Minimum Striking Surface Width for Heavy Mass Weapons – The minimum width of all heavy mass blades and bludgeons shall be not less than 3½ inches.  The blade or bludgeon must meet the minimum padding requirements for all striking surface.  The end of the blade or bludgeon must conform to the Larger than an Eye Socket rule.

Minimum Striking Surface Length – The minimum length of the striking surface of all blade and bludgeons is 6 inches.  The edge of all blades and bludgeons must conform to the Larger than an Eye Socket rule.

Minimum Striking Surface Cross-Curvature – The minimum curvature for a striking surface of a round blade or bludgeon (omni-blade) is a 2½ inches diameter cylinder across the blade face (thickness dimension).

Minimum Striking Surface Edge-Curvature – The minimum curvature along the length of a striking surface is a 2½-inch diameter circle.

Minimum Striking Surface Edge Angles – The minimum angle formed by the foam of the weapon along the length of a striking surface edge may be no less than 90-degrees (not sharper than 90 degrees).  All angles between 90 degrees and 135-degrees must be rounded or have a ½-inch bevel cut at the center of the angle. Striking surface angles that meet these requirements do not have to conform to the Larger than an Eye Socket rule.

Beveled Blade Edges – A weapon blade may contain a beveled along the edge. The beveled edge must be constructed of open cell foam.  It must be no sharper than 90 degrees and a minimum 45 degrees from the directions of swing.
